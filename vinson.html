    Panda

    Pandas are iconic black and white bears native to China, primarily known for their bamboo diet and distinctive appearance. They are gentle, solitary creatures and symbols of conservation efforts, beloved worldwide for their cute and cuddly appearance, making them one of the most recognized and cherished animals on the planet.

    Penguin

    Penguins are flightless birds, famous for their black and white plumage. They thrive in cold waters of the Southern Hemisphere and the Arctic. These adept swimmers feed primarily on fish and krill. Penguins often form large colonies, displaying social behavior. They're symbols of polar regions and conservation efforts globally.

    Kangoroo

    Kangaroos are iconic Australian marsupials, known for their powerful hind legs and distinctive hopping movement. They carry and nurture their young, called joeys, in pouches. Kangaroos inhabit various environments and play a vital role in Australian ecosystems and are a wonderous animal that we have in our zoo.

    Gorilla

    Gorillas are the largest primates, known for their immense strength and gentle nature. They inhabit forests in Central and East Africa. Gorilla groups, led by dominant males, are close-knit families. These highly intelligent creatures are herbivorous, with a diet comprising leaves, stems, and fruit. They are a symbol of primate conservation.
