    Size & Weight

    Life expectancy: 60-70 years

    Height: 9 feet to 10 feet | Length: 18 feet to 21 feet

    Weight: Up to 4,000 pounds

    Fun Facts!

    1) There are fibrous pads on their feet that cushion each step they take. It acts as a shock absorber to protect from injury due to their heavy body.

    2) They have incredible memory. Their temporal lobe-area in brain associated with memory- is larger and denser than humans.

    3) They communicate with tumpet calls, body language, touch and sent. They can communicate through vibrations in the ground!

    About!

    Elephants are one of the largest living land animals. They have massive bodies, large ears, and a large trunk. There are three living species currently recognized: African bush, African forest, and Asian elephant. African savannah elephants are the largest of the species, with the African forest and Asian elelphants are smaller. African elephants grow tusks that serve a variety of purposes: protection, move things, and gather food. Populations have been experience significant decline over the last century due to poachers.
