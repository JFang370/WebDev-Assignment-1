    Giraffes

    Giraffa camelopardalis

    Type: Mammal Family: Giraffidae

    Genus: Giraffa

    Subspecies: 9

    Size & Weight

    Life expectancy: 20-25 years

    Height: 14 feet to 19 feet

    Weight: 4,200 pounds (males) / 2,400 pounds (female)

    Fun Facts!

    1) Giraffes are on constant alert, only sleeping about 20 minutes a night.

    2) Giraffes have hearts the size of basketballs.

    3) Due to their long legs-over 6 feet tall, they can move at speeds of 35 miles an hour.

    About!

    Giraffes come from the African savanna area. They are the tallest mammals on earth. A giraffe can be as tall as 14 feet to 19 feet! All giraffes are tan with spotted coats. Their coat colors depend on what they consume and where they live. Each one of them has a different pattern, making them unique. Giraffes are capable of making sounds like: moos, roars, hisses, and grunts-rarely, however! Giraffes are often early warning signals for wildlife, Giraffes are capable of resting while standing up, but can also lie down-though they are vulnerable when they do.
